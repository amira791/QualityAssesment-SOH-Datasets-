300 ELECTRIC VEHICLES REAL-WORLD BMS DATASET QUALITY SCORING LOGIC
==================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
The scoring system evaluates the 300 EV Real-World BMS Dataset against 6 quality criteria,
each with specific aspects relevant to large-scale fleet BMS data. Scores range from "++" 
(Fully Satisfied) to "-" (Not Satisfied), with "N/A" for non-applicable checks.

Dataset context: 300 real-world electric vehicles with NCM batteries (96 cells in series, 
155 Ah rated capacity). 3 years of continuous operation at 0.1 Hz sampling. Includes real 
driving patterns, charging events, and diverse operating conditions. Largest public EV 
fleet dataset available.

SCORING THRESHOLDS & LOGIC
==========================

1. CORRECTNESS (Physical plausibility + signal semantics)
---------------------------------------------------------

1a. Physical plausibility (voltage, current, SOC, temperature): ++/+/-
    - Voltage bounds: 250-420V (typical EV pack, 96× 2.5-4.3V cells)
    - Current bounds: -400A to +400A (covers driving discharge to fast charging)
    - SOC bounds: 0-100% (theoretical), but real data shows 0-127%
    - Temperature bounds: -20°C to 60°C (operational range)
    - Calculates % of measurements outside physical limits
    - Score "++" when <0.1% outliers across all signals
    - Score "+" when <5% outliers (minor, explainable anomalies)
    - Score "o" when 5-10% outliers
    - Score "-" when >10% outliers
    - 300EV dataset: SOC outliers ~1-3% (mostly >100% due to regenerative braking/
      calibration), current/voltage outliers minimal (<0.1%), temperature within bounds → "+"

1b. Signal semantics: ++/+/-
    - Verifies current polarity (negative=discharge/driving, positive=charge)
    - Checks SOC direction (decreases during driving, increases during charging)
    - Validates voltage-SOC correlation (higher voltage = higher SOC)
    - Score "++" when semantics are correct across all vehicles
    - Score "+" when minor inconsistencies exist
    - 300EV dataset: Excellent semantic consistency, SOC>100% is explainable 
      (regenerative braking, sensor calibration) → "++"

2. COMPLETENESS
---------------

2a. Missing values: ++/+/-
    - Calculates vehicles with missing data and overall missing percentage
    - Score "++" when 0% missing across all vehicles
    - Score "+" when <5% missing overall (manageable for fleet analysis)
    - Score "o" when 5-15% missing
    - Score "-" when >15% missing
    - 300EV dataset: Some vehicles have telemetry gaps, but critical signals 
      (SOC, current, voltage) present for most → "+"

2b. Operational mode coverage: ++/+/-
    - Checks coverage of driving, charging, and rest modes across fleet
    - Score "++" when all three modes present with good representation
    - Score "+" when all modes present but imbalanced
    - Score "o" when missing one mode
    - 300EV dataset: Excellent coverage, SOC from 0-127%, captures full 
      operational envelope → "++"

3. ANOMALY MINIMIZATION
-----------------------

3a. Statistical outliers per vehicle: ++/+/-
    - Evaluates SOC outliers (especially >100%), current/voltage extremes
    - Score "++" when <10% of vehicles have outliers
    - Score "+" when 10-30% of vehicles have outliers (minor, explainable)
    - Score "o" when 30-50% have outliers
    - Score "-" when >50% have outliers
    - 300EV dataset: SOC outliers in many vehicles but explainable 
      (regenerative braking), current/voltage outliers rare → "+"

3b. Signal consistency: ++/+/-
    - Checks cross-signal physical consistency across fleet
    - Verifies: current integration ≈ SOC changes, voltage-SOC relationship
    - Scores "++" when consistent across >95% of vehicles
    - Scores "+" when consistent across >80% of vehicles
    - 300EV dataset: Physically consistent behavior, no systematic drift,
      SOC>100% is the main anomaly but follows from definition → "++"

4. REPRESENTATIVENESS & DIVERSITY
---------------------------------

4a. Fleet size: ++/+/-
    - Evaluates number of vehicles for statistical significance
    - Score "++" when ≥100 vehicles (large-scale statistical power)
    - Score "+" when 50-99 vehicles
    - Score "o" when 20-49 vehicles
    - Score "-" when <20 vehicles
    - 300EV dataset: 300 vehicles → "++"
      (Largest public EV dataset, excellent for ML generalization)

4b. Battery chemistry: ++/+/-
    - Counts distinct battery chemistries
    - Score "++" when ≥2 major chemistries represented
    - Score "+" when 1 chemistry with variations
    - Score "o" when single chemistry only
    - Score "-" when unknown
    - 300EV dataset: Single chemistry (NCM) → "o"
      (Consistent for focused analysis, limits cross-chemistry comparison)

4c. Temporal coverage: ++/+/-
    - Evaluates data collection duration
    - Score "++" when >12 months (captures seasonal effects, degradation)
    - Score "+" when 6-12 months
    - Score "o" when 3-6 months
    - Score "-" when <3 months
    - 300EV dataset: 3 years continuous → "++"
      (Excellent for seasonal patterns, long-term degradation)

4d. Real-world operation: ++/+/-
    - Assesses authenticity of operational data
    - Score "++" when real-world fleet data (not simulated)
    - Score "+" when mostly realistic with minor limitations
    - Score "o" when synthetic or controlled
    - 300EV dataset: Actual EV fleet, real drivers, routes, charging → "++"

4e. Cell-level data: ++/+/-
    - Checks availability of individual cell voltages
    - Score "++" when full cell-level readings (all 96 cells)
    - Score "+" when max/min cell voltages available
    - Score "-" when only pack-level data
    - 300EV dataset: Max/min cell voltages available (detected), 
      sufficient for imbalance analysis but lacks full cell resolution → "+"

4f. Sampling frequency: ++/+/-
    - Evaluates temporal resolution for intended analyses
    - Score "++" when ≥1 Hz (captures transients)
    - Score "+" when 0.5-1 Hz (good for most analyses)
    - Score "o" when 0.1-0.5 Hz (adequate for operational patterns)
    - Score "-" when <0.1 Hz (too sparse)
    - 300EV dataset: 0.1 Hz (10 seconds) → "o"
      (Good for SOC, driving cycles, charging events; insufficient for transients)

5. BALANCED DISTRIBUTION
------------------------

5a. SOC range coverage: ++/+/-
    - Analyzes SOC utilization across fleet
    - Score "++" when full range 0-100% covered
    - Score "+" when 80-100% coverage
    - Score "o" when 50-80% coverage
    - Score "-" when <50% coverage
    - 300EV dataset: SOC from 0-127% (full coverage with outliers),
      includes deep discharge and overcharge regions → "++"
      (Though >100% may indicate calibration issues)

5b. Vehicle data balance: ++/+/-
    - Calculates CV of data points across vehicles
    - Score "++" when CV < 0.3 (well-balanced)
    - Score "+" when CV 0.3-0.6 (moderately balanced)
    - Score "o" when CV 0.6-1.0 (imbalanced)
    - Score "-" when CV > 1.0 (highly imbalanced)
    - 300EV dataset: CV depends on usage intensity, some imbalance
      expected (different driving frequencies), typically 0.4-0.6 → "+"

5c. Operating condition coverage: ++/+/-
    - Evaluates diversity of current, temperature, speed conditions
    - Score "++" when wide range covering most real-world conditions
    - Score "+" when good coverage with some gaps
    - Score "o" when limited range
    - 300EV dataset: Current from -400A to +400A (full range),
      diverse temperatures, captures urban/highway/fast charging → "++"

6. TEMPORAL COHERENCE
---------------------

6a. Timestamp ordering: ++/+/-
    - Checks chronological ordering within each vehicle
    - Score "++" when strictly increasing for all vehicles
    - Score "+" when minor issues but fixable
    - 300EV dataset: Sequential timestamps, monotonic mileage → "++"

6b. Temporal resolution: ++/+/-
    - Evaluates consistency of sampling intervals
    - Score "++" when regular, predictable intervals
    - Score "+" when mostly regular with minor variations
    - Score "o" when irregular but usable
    - 300EV dataset: Consistent 10-second intervals (0.1 Hz) → "+"

6c. Long-term consistency: ++/+/-
    - Checks for consistent data collection over time
    - Score "++" when protocol unchanged, no gaps or drift
    - Score "+" when minor gaps but consistent otherwise
    - Score "o" when significant gaps or changes
    - 300EV dataset: 3 years consistent monitoring, captures 
      seasonal and degradation trends → "++"

AGGREGATION RULES
=================

The final scorecard presents each aspect individually (no single composite score)
to provide granular quality insights. This allows users to:
- Identify specific strengths (fleet size, temporal coverage = "++")
- Recognize limitations (chemistry diversity, sampling frequency = "o")
- Make informed decisions about fitness for specific use cases

INTERPRETATION GUIDE
====================

"++" (Fully Satisfied):  
  - Exceeds expectations for this aspect
  - Examples: 300 vehicles, 3 years data, real-world operation
  - Gold standard for fleet-scale EV datasets

"+" (Mostly Satisfied):  
  - Meets core requirements for most applications
  - Minor issues present but manageable
  - Examples: SOC outliers (1-3%), some missing data, cell-level max/min only
  - Suitable for most EV fleet analytics after light cleaning

"o" (Partially Satisfied):  
  - Significant limitation for some use cases
  - Examples: single chemistry (NCM), 0.1Hz sampling
  - Acceptable for SOC/energy analysis but not for transient studies

"-" (Not Satisfied):  
  - Missing important feature or capability
  - Examples: none in this dataset (all aspects at least "o")
  - Would indicate major quality issues

"N/A" (Not Applicable):  
  - Criterion cannot be evaluated
  - Missing required data or context

KEY ASSUMPTIONS
===============

1. Physical bounds derived from NCM battery pack (96 series cells, 2.5-4.3V per cell)
2. Current bounds -400A to +400A represent realistic EV operation
3. SOC >100% due to regenerative braking overcharge or sensor calibration
4. 0.1 Hz sampling (10 seconds) adequate for fleet-level BMS analysis
5. Cell imbalance detectable from min/max cell voltages
6. 3 years sufficient for degradation trend analysis
7. Fleet of 300 vehicles provides statistical power for ML
8. Missing data due to telemetry gaps (real-world constraint)
9. Temperature within -20°C to 60°C operational range
10. Consistent data definitions across all vehicles in fleet

SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS FOR 300EV DATASET
=========================================

1. Largest Public EV Dataset: 300 vehicles - unmatched in scale
   - Enables robust statistical analysis
   - Deep learning with good generalization
   - Fleet-level pattern discovery

2. Longest Temporal Coverage: 3 years continuous
   - Seasonal pattern analysis (summer vs winter)
   - Battery degradation tracking
   - Long-term usage trend analysis
   - Only dataset with multi-year real-world operation

3. Real-World Authenticity: Pure operational data
   - No laboratory artifacts
   - Real driver behavior variability
   - Actual charging infrastructure usage
   - True environmental conditions

4. SOC Outliers (0-127%):
   - SOC >100% likely from regenerative braking overcharge events
   - May indicate calibration issues or actual overcharging
   - Needs filtering for strict battery modeling
   - Acceptable for relative comparisons

5. Sampling Limitations (0.1 Hz):
   - 10-second intervals miss sub-second transients
   - Cannot capture:
     * Fast voltage/current spikes
     * Cell-level dynamics
     * Detailed degradation mechanisms
   - Adequate for:
     * SOC tracking
     * Driving cycle characterization
     * Charging pattern analysis
     * Fleet-level aggregation

6. Chemistry Uniformity: Single chemistry (NCM)
   - Advantage: Consistent for focused NCM research
   - Disadvantage: No cross-chemistry comparison
   - Missing LFP, NCA, LCO comparisons

7. Cell-Level Resolution: Limited
   - Only max/min cell voltages available
   - Cannot analyze individual cell degradation
   - Cannot detect specific weak cells
   - Sufficient for imbalance detection

8. Telemetry Gaps: Real-world reality
   - Some vehicles have missing data (connectivity issues)
   - Critical BMS signals usually present
   - Acceptable for fleet-level analysis

9. Data Volume Challenge: 300 vehicles × 3 years × 0.1Hz
   - Approximately 300 × 3 × 365 × 24 × 3600 / 10 ≈ 2.8 billion rows
   - Requires big data infrastructure
   - Quality scoring uses sampling (pragmatic approach)

VALIDATION NOTES
================

- 300EV dataset strengths: scale (300 vehicles), duration (3 years), authenticity
- Key limitations: sampling rate (0.1 Hz), single chemistry, SOC outliers, cell-level resolution
- Designed for: Fleet analytics, ML, usage pattern analysis, degradation trends
- Not suitable for: High-frequency dynamics, detailed cell modeling, multi-chemistry studies
- Most comprehensive EV fleet dataset publicly available
- Perfect for bridging laboratory aging to real-world operation at scale
- Complementary to laboratory datasets (MIT, Oxford, CALCE, SNL)
- Data quality is good given real-world constraints requiring some cleaning
