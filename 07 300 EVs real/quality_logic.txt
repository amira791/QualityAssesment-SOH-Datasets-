REAL-WORLD EV OPERATION DATASET QUALITY SCORING LOGIC
======================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
The scoring system evaluates the Real-World Electric Vehicle Operation Dataset against 6 quality criteria,
each with specific aspects relevant to operational fleet data. Scores range from "++" (Fully Satisfied) 
to "-" (Not Satisfied), with "N/A" for non-applicable checks.

Dataset context: 10 real-world EVs (6 passenger NCM, 1 passenger LFP, 3 electric buses LFP) with 
1 month of operational data each. Total 1,188,470 data points capturing driving, charging, and rest 
periods. Diverse conditions including real-world driving patterns, fast charging, and varying SOC ranges.

SCORING THRESHOLDS & LOGIC
==========================

1. CORRECTNESS (Physical plausibility + signal semantics)
---------------------------------------------------------

1a. Physical plausibility (voltage, current, SOC, temperature, speed): ++/+/-
    - Voltage bounds: 200-800V (typical EV battery pack voltages)
    - Current bounds: -500A to 500A (discharge to charge, covers fast charging)
    - SOC bounds: 0-100% (absolute scale)
    - Temperature bounds: -20°C to 60°C (vehicle operating range)
    - Speed bound: 0-150 km/h (legal highway speeds)
    - Calculates % of measurements outside physical limits
    - Score "++" when <0.1% outliers overall
    - Score "+" when <5% outliers (acceptable with cleaning)
    - Score "o" when 5-10% outliers
    - Score "-" when >10% outliers
    - EV dataset: voltage outliers 0.003% (minor, Vehicle#9 up to 1310V), 
      current 0.002%, SOC 0.0004%, temperature 0.016% (Vehicle#9 255°C), 
      speed 0.008% → "+" (minor issues in Vehicle#9)

1b. Signal semantics: ++/+/-
    - Verifies charging signal encoding (1=charging, 3=driving)
    - Checks current polarity (positive=charge, negative=discharge)
    - Validates SOC physical consistency
    - Score "++" when semantics are correct and documented
    - Score "+" when minor inconsistencies exist
    - EV dataset: perfect semantic encoding across all vehicles → "++"

2. COMPLETENESS
---------------

2a. Missing values: ++/+/-
    - Calculates vehicles with missing data and overall missing percentage
    - Score "++" when 0% missing across all vehicles
    - Score "+" when <1% missing (minor, manageable)
    - Score "o" when 1-5% missing
    - Score "-" when >5% missing
    - EV dataset: Vehicle#8 only vehicle with missing (0.56% in 6 columns),
      others 0% missing, overall completeness 99.95%+ → "+"

2b. Operational mode coverage: ++/+/-
    - Checks coverage of driving, charging, and rest modes
    - Score "++" when all three modes present across fleet
    - Score "+" when most vehicles have all modes
    - Score "o" when limited mode coverage
    - EV dataset: excellent coverage except Vehicle#7 (only charging in sample),
      SOC ranges 1-100% across fleet → "+"

3. ANOMALY MINIMIZATION
-----------------------

3a. Statistical outliers per vehicle: ++/+/-
    - Evaluates outlier presence per vehicle (especially Vehicle#9)
    - Score "++" when 0 vehicles have significant outliers
    - Score "+" when 1 vehicle has minor outliers
    - Score "o" when 1 vehicle has major outliers (needs cleaning)
    - Score "-" when multiple vehicles have outlier issues
    - EV dataset: Vehicle#9 has significant anomalies (1310V, 255°C),
      likely sensor errors, other vehicles 0% outliers → "o"

3b. Signal consistency: ++/+/-
    - Checks physical relationships between signals
    - Verifies: current→SOC direction, voltage→SOC relationship
    - Checks temperature response to charging
    - Score "++" when all signals physically consistent
    - Score "+" when minor inconsistencies but explainable
    - Score "o" when some vehicles show inconsistencies
    - EV dataset: all signals consistent except Vehicle#9 anomalies → "+"

4. REPRESENTATIVENESS & DIVERSITY
---------------------------------

4a. Vehicle type diversity: ++/+/-
    - Counts distinct vehicle types (passenger, bus, commercial)
    - Score "++" when ≥3 types or includes both passenger and commercial
    - Score "+" when 2 types
    - Score "o" when 1 type
    - EV dataset: passenger vehicles (7) + electric buses (3) → "++"

4b. Chemistry diversity: ++/+/-
    - Counts distinct battery chemistries
    - Score "++" when ≥2 major chemistries represented
    - Score "+" when 1 chemistry with variations
    - Score "o" when single chemistry only
    - EV dataset: NCM (6 vehicles) + LFP (4 vehicles) → "++"

4c. Capacity range: ++/+/-
    - Evaluates spread of battery capacities
    - Score "++" when range >3× or covers multiple vehicle segments
    - Score "+" when good spread but limited
    - Score "o" when narrow range
    - EV dataset: 120Ah (passenger LFP) to 645Ah (bus LFP), 5.4× range → "++"

4d. Real-world operation: ++/+/-
    - Assesses authenticity of operational data
    - Score "++" when real-world driving/charging data (not lab-simulated)
    - Score "+" when mostly realistic but some limitations
    - Score "o" when simulated or synthetic
    - EV dataset: actual EV fleet data with real driving patterns, 
      fast charging, city/highway mix → "++"

4e. Sampling frequency: ++/+/-
    - Evaluates temporal resolution for intended analyses
    - Score "++" when ≥1 Hz (captures transients)
    - Score "+" when 0.5-1 Hz (good for most analyses)
    - Score "o" when 0.1-0.5 Hz (adequate for operational patterns)
    - Score "-" when <0.1 Hz (too sparse)
    - EV dataset: 0.1Hz (9 vehicles), 0.5Hz (Vehicle#7), 10-second sampling
      adequate for driving/charging patterns but not transients → "o"

4f. Temporal coverage: ++/+/-
    - Evaluates data collection duration
    - Score "++" when >6 months (captures seasonal effects)
    - Score "+" when 3-6 months
    - Score "o" when 1-3 months
    - Score "-" when <1 month
    - EV dataset: 1 month per vehicle → "o"
      (sufficient for usage patterns, insufficient for degradation studies)

5. BALANCED DISTRIBUTION
------------------------

5a. SOC range coverage: ++/+/-
    - Analyzes SOC utilization across fleet
    - Score "++" when full range 0-100% covered, good distribution
    - Score "+" when wide range but some bias
    - Score "o" when limited range
    - EV dataset: SOC min 1-46%, max 97-100% across vehicles,
      means 60.9-78.3%, excellent full-range coverage → "++"

5b. Vehicle data balance: ++/+/-
    - Calculates CV of data points across vehicles
    - Score "++" when CV < 0.3 (well-balanced)
    - Score "+" when CV 0.3-0.6 (moderately balanced)
    - Score "o" when CV > 0.6 (imbalanced)
    - EV dataset: Vehicle#7 has more data (0.5Hz vs 0.1Hz),
      CV ~0.4, reasonable balance → "+"

5c. Chemistry balance: ++/+/-
    - Checks balance between NCM and LFP representation
    - Score "++" when both chemistries have comparable data volume
    - Score "+" when both present but imbalanced
    - Score "o" when one dominates
    - EV dataset: NCM (6 vehicles, passenger), LFP (4 vehicles, passenger+bus),
      good representation for comparison studies → "++"

6. TEMPORAL COHERENCE
---------------------

6a. Timestamp ordering: ++/+/-
    - Checks chronological ordering of timestamps within each vehicle
    - Score "++" when strictly increasing for all vehicles
    - Score "+" when minor issues but fixable
    - EV dataset: all vehicles have sequential timestamps → "++"

6b. Temporal gaps: ++/+/-
    - Evaluates regularity of sampling intervals
    - Score "++" when regular intervals, no significant gaps
    - Score "+" when minor gaps (<1% of intervals)
    - Score "o" when irregular but continuous
    - EV dataset: regular 10-second (0.1Hz) or 2-second (0.5Hz) intervals,
      continuous monitoring, no significant gaps → "++"

6c. Mileage progression: ++/+/-
    - Verifies cumulative odometer increases monotonically
    - Score "++" when strictly increasing for all vehicles
    - Score "+" when minor issues
    - EV dataset: mileage monotonically increasing, 27,677km to 387,325km
      total distance, excellent progression tracking → "++"

6d. Event identification: ++/+/-
    - Checks ability to identify driving/charging events
    - Score "++" when events clearly distinguishable
    - Score "+" when mostly clear but some ambiguity
    - EV dataset: charging_signal and current clearly define events,
      SOC evolution follows expected patterns → "++"

AGGREGATION RULES
=================

The final scorecard presents each aspect individually (no single composite score)
to provide granular quality insights. This allows users to:
- Identify specific strengths (real-world data, vehicle diversity, temporal coherence = "++")
- Recognize limitations (sampling frequency, temporal coverage = "o")
- Make informed decisions about fitness for specific use cases

INTERPRETATION GUIDE
====================

"++" (Fully Satisfied):  
  - Exceeds expectations for this aspect
  - Examples: chemistry diversity, SOC coverage, temporal coherence
  - Gold standard for real-world operational data

"+" (Mostly Satisfied):  
  - Meets core requirements for most applications
  - Minor issues present but manageable
  - Examples: physical plausibility (Vehicle#9 outliers), missing data (0.56%)
  - Suitable for most EV operation analysis after light cleaning

"o" (Partially Satisfied):  
  - Significant limitation for some use cases
  - Examples: sampling frequency (0.1Hz), temporal coverage (1 month)
  - Acceptable for operational pattern analysis but not transient dynamics

"-" (Not Satisfied):  
  - Missing important feature or capability
  - Examples: none in this dataset (all aspects at least "o")
  - Would indicate severe quality issues

"N/A" (Not Applicable):  
  - Criterion cannot be evaluated
  - Missing required data or context

KEY ASSUMPTIONS
===============

1. Physical bounds derived from typical EV battery packs and vehicle operation
2. Charging signal encoding: 1=charging, 3=driving (documented in data description)
3. Current polarity: positive=charging, negative=discharging
4. 1 month per vehicle sufficient for usage pattern analysis but not degradation
5. 0.1Hz sampling (10 seconds) adequate for driving/charging events
6. Vehicle#9 outliers (1310V, 255°C) are sensor errors requiring filtering
7. Vehicle#7 only has charging mode in 1-month sample (possible data collection period)
8. All vehicles use same signal definitions despite different types
9. Missing data in Vehicle#8 is random (0.56%, 6 columns)
10. Mileage is cumulative odometer (monotonic increasing)

SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS FOR EV DATASET
======================================

1. Real-World Uniqueness: Only dataset in battery collection with actual field data
   - Not laboratory-controlled cycles
   - Includes real driving patterns, traffic, weather effects
   - Captures realistic fast charging behavior

2. Vehicle Diversity: Unmatched diversity in single dataset
   - Passenger cars (NCM + LFP)
   - Electric buses (LFP, different capacities)
   - Different usage patterns (personal vs fleet)

3. Operational Realism: Perfect for operational research
   - SOC management strategies
   - Charging behavior analysis
   - Driving pattern characterization
   - Fleet analytics

4. Sampling Limitations: 0.1Hz insufficient for:
   - High-frequency transient analysis
   - Detailed voltage/current dynamics
   - Instantaneous power calculations
   - Cell-level modeling

5. Temporal Limitations: 1 month per vehicle
   - Cannot study battery degradation over time
   - No seasonal variation captured
   - Limited for long-term trend analysis
   - Perfect for operational patterns, not aging

6. Vehicle#9 Anomalies: Requires cleaning
   - 1310V likely sensor scaling error
   - 255°C clearly erroneous (impossible temperature)
   - Affects <0.02% of data
   - Easily filterable

7. Missing Data: Vehicle#8 only
   - 0.56% missing in 6 columns
   - Minimal impact on analysis
   - Can be dropped or interpolated

8. Chemistry Comparison: Unique opportunity
   - NCM vs LFP in real operation
   - Different vehicle types (passenger vs bus)
   - Realistic comparison of chemistry behaviors

VALIDATION NOTES
================

- EV dataset strengths: real-world, diverse vehicles/chemistries, excellent temporal coherence
- Key limitations: sampling rate (0.1Hz), duration (1 month), Vehicle#9 outliers
- Designed for: EV operation analysis, fleet management, charging behavior, SOC strategies
- Not suitable for: degradation modeling, high-frequency dynamics, cell-level studies
- Most "real" dataset in the collection - captures actual EV usage
- Complementary to laboratory aging datasets (MIT, Oxford, CALCE, SNL)
- Perfect for bridging gap between laboratory aging and real-world operation
- Data quality is high despite minor issues requiring cleaning
